The Story

From incentives to outcomes — an analyst built on both sides of the table.

Almost every decision I've made as an analyst started in the same uncomfortable place: a noisy, incomplete signal, a business that needed an answer before the data was clean, and a real consequence attached to getting it right. I've spent years learning to be calm there.

My foundation is economics — at The American College I learned to look at any market and ask who has the incentive to do what, and which lever, if it moved, would move everything else. A digital marketing diploma added the demand side.

"A number on a dashboard is never the point. The behavior it represents — and the lever that changes it — is the point."

At Rayappan Productions I owned royalty operations, then ran the experiments that lifted first-week streams. Around Saregama India I built a single comparable factbase across four streaming platforms. As founder of Thirdeye Records I run the whole analytics-and-operations loop with my own money on the line.

My MBA in Business Analytics (3.85 GPA) and my IEEE-published research formalized the craft. My differentiator isn't being the most technical person in the room; it's knowing what a recommendation has to look like to actually get used — because I've been the person deciding whether to use it.

It taught me the founder's version of the analyst's discipline: every decision has a cost, every number has an owner — and the owner is me.
