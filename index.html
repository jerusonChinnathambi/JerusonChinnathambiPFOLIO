Flagship case study · Consulting engagement · Jan–May 2026

Biryani Heaven.
From POS data to a $22K monthly lift.

A full-stack revenue-intelligence engagement for an independent restaurant: a longitudinal analysis of 4,936 point-of-sale transactions across 126 operating days, a six-model machine-learning forecasting framework, and an evidence-based growth program whose execution coincided with $54,000+ in May revenue — a $22,000+ incremental gain over the April baseline of $32,422.

01 · PROBLEM FORMULATIONStrong volume, zero analytical visibility.

The client had accumulated five months of daily POS records but possessed no framework for interpreting them — no understanding of which trading windows underperformed, what mechanisms drove weekend outperformance, or how to plan staffing and inventory with statistical confidence. The engagement was therefore scoped around four research objectives: characterize historical revenue structure and day-of-week dynamics; identify and quantify avoidable revenue losses; construct a validated forecasting system suitable for operational planning; and design data-grounded interventions targeting 15–25% revenue growth over six months.

02 · EXPLORATORY ANALYSISA 41% weekend premium — and $27,719 left on the table.

Exploratory analysis of $158,753 in net revenue revealed pronounced structural regularities. Weekend trading days averaged $1,564 versus $1,108 on weekdays — a 41% premium decomposable into higher order volume (+30%) and larger average tickets (+10%). Monday emerged as the weakest open day at $933, running 45% below the Saturday peak. Most consequentially, the policy of closing on Tuesdays — 22 closures over the study period — was estimated to represent $27,719 in foregone revenue. An anomalous observation on January 13 ($59.96) was isolated via Z-score screening (|z| > 2.0) and excluded as a partial-day event.

03 · MODELING & FORECASTINGSix models. One validated system.

Five monthly Excel POS exports were parsed with dynamic header detection, then transformed into 34 engineered features spanning lag structures, rolling statistics, cyclical encodings, and calendar effects. Six candidate models were trained on 100 open days (Jan 1 – Apr 25) and evaluated against a strictly held-out 26-day window (Apr 26 – May 25), with TimeSeriesSplit cross-validation (k = 4) guarding against temporal leakage. Gradient Boosting was selected on out-of-sample performance — R² = 0.761, MAPE 9.7%, MAE $157 — and deployed in a 60/40 ensemble with Prophet, projecting $35,544 for June 2026.

End-to-end forecasting architecture — data flows continuously from raw POS exports through feature engineering, parallel model training and holdout validation, into the production ensemble.

04 · STRATEGY, EXECUTION & OUTCOMESeven interventions. One measurable result.

Seven growth strategies were derived directly from the empirical findings — each intervention mapped to a specific quantified insight rather than intuition. Monday and Wednesday, the two weakest trading windows, were targeted with paid Google Ads and organic social campaigns; online ordering and corporate catering were activated as entirely untapped channels (zero delivery revenue across five months of data); weekend staff were trained on upsell protocols; and tip-prompt optimization addressed a 2.7% observed tip rate against a 15–18% industry norm. Modeled potential across all seven interventions: $8,510–$15,410 per month. The realized result exceeded the program's near-term targets.
